[Ends June 2021] 70 Free Certificates From Coursera for a Limited Time

The limited-time offer includes courses from Google, AWS, Duke, Michigan, Yale, and more.

Pat Bowden & Suparn Patra
May 12th, 2021
  • Facebook
  • Twitter
  • Envelope
  • 68

The COVID-19 lockdowns led to unprecedented interest in online courses. In late March 2020, Coursera responded by introducing 100 free certificates . Initially, these were to remain free until the end of May. But the offer proved so successful for Coursera, that it was extended — first until July 31, then through 2020.

But things didn’t end there. To kick off 2021, Coursera allowed learners to redeem a free certificate from a list of 25+ courses. And in late March, they announced a list of ~20 free certificate courses to the celebrate Holi, the Indian festival of colours. Then in April, to celebrate the company’s 9th birthday, they offered 9 free certificate courses.

New Limited-Time Offer

Now, to celebrate Pride Month, Coursera is offering free certificates for ten courses until June 30, 2021 which we listed below.

In addition to these courses, 58 more courses with with free certificates are listed below, also valid until June 30, 2021. Although this offer mentions it is only for India, here at Class Central, we have been able to access the free certificates from other countries as well.

The promotion includes courses from Duke, Michigan, Google Cloud, AWS, and Yale, among others.

Some courses about COVID-19 from Johns Hopkins University and Stanford University also currently have free certificates. As always, all offers are subject to change.

(We’ll continue to update this page with Coursera’s new free certificate offers. So you might want to bookmark it!)

How to Redeem a Free Course?

To redeem a course:

  1. Click on the course name in the list below.
  2. Click on the Go to Class button, on the top right.
  3. Wait for the promo bar to appear at the top.
  4. Click on Enroll for Free.
  5. Log in or join Coursera.
  6. Verify that course fee has been waived, like on the image below.
